Here is a structured, comprehensive questionnaire designed specifically to test the **Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)** capabilities of Vidhaan AI. 

These questions are tailored to target the specific statutory acts in your local database (such as the *Constitution of India*, *Indian Contract Act*, *Arbitration and Conciliation Act*, etc.) to help you verify dense vector retrieval, sparse keyword search, and parametric fallback systems.

---

### Category A: Direct Statutory Matches (Testing Exact Section/Article Retrieval)
*These test the hybrid search's ability to locate exact sections when specific names or numbers are provided.*

1.  **Question**: *What are the grounds for challenging the appointment of an arbitrator under Section 12 of the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996?*
    *   **RAG Expectation**: The "Thinking" panel should open, show RAG status, retrieve chunks from `Arbitration_and_Conciliation_Act_1996.pdf` containing Section 12, and display the PDF name.
2.  **Question**: *Explain the definition of a contract of indemnity under Section 124 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872.*
    *   **RAG Expectation**: The system should retrieve the exact text of Section 124 of the Contract Act and explain the relationship between the indemnifier and the indemnity holder.
3.  **Question**: *How is Equality before Law defined under Article 14 of the Constitution of India?*
    *   **RAG Expectation**: The system should fetch constitutional vector chunks, highlight Article 14, and explain standard legal interpretations of "Equality before Law" and "Equal Protection of the Laws."

---

### Category B: Semantic Concept Queries (Testing Dense Embedding Match *Without* Citations)
*These test if the RAG vector engine can find the correct legal section based on legal concepts, even when you do not mention any section or article numbers.*

4.  **Question**: *What is the legal recourse if a party promises to save another party from loss caused by the conduct of the promisor?*
    *   **RAG Expectation**: Even without mentioning "Section 124" or "Indemnity", the dense vector search should semantically match this to **Section 124 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872** (Contract of Indemnity) and display the relevant Contract Act PDF in the sources drawer.
5.  **Question**: *Under what circumstances can a court appoint an arbitrator if the parties fail to agree on a procedure?*
    *   **RAG Expectation**: The dense vector search should locate **Section 11** of the Arbitration and Conciliation Act (Appointment of Arbitrators) and list the Arbitration Act PDF.
6.  **Question**: *Can the State make special provisions for women and children, or does that violate the right to non-discrimination?*
    *   **RAG Expectation**: The engine should semantically map this to **Article 15(3)** of the Constitution of India (Special provisions for women and children) and show that it is an exception to the general rule of non-discrimination.

---

### Category C: Complex Statutory Interpretation (Testing Synthesizing and Reasoning)
*These test how the LLM synthesizes a structured, disclaimer-free answer when multiple overlapping chunks are retrieved.*

7.  **Question**: *Compare the difference between a Contract of Indemnity and a Contract of Guarantee under the Indian Contract Act.*
    *   **RAG Expectation**: The system should retrieve chunks for Section 124 (Indemnity) and Section 126 (Guarantee), and synthesize a clean comparative structure or table outlining the number of parties, nature of liability, and primary objectives.
8.  **Question**: *Under what grounds can an arbitral award be set aside by a court under the Arbitration Act?*
    *   **RAG Expectation**: The hybrid search should fetch chunks related to **Section 34** of the Arbitration Act, presenting a highly structured list of grounds (e.g., incapacity of parties, invalid arbitration agreement, public policy violation).

---

### Category D: Parametric Fallback (Testing Out-of-Scope RAG Resilience)
*These test how the system behaves when the query is legal but falls outside the specific ingested statutory database chunks, ensuring it falls back gracefully to LLM knowledge without crashing.*

9.  **Question**: *What is the difference between a Cognizable and Non-Cognizable offense under Indian criminal law?*
    *   **RAG Expectation**: If the criminal acts are not fully indexed, the "Thinking" logs should indicate that no high-confidence database records matched, fall back to standard parametric memory, and synthesize a structured, dispassionate legal explanation of the differences under the CrPC/BNSS.
10. **Question**: *What are the tax slabs under the Indian Income Tax Act for the assessment year 2026?*
    *   **RAG Expectation**: Acknowledges that the specific current Finance Act/Tax slabs are not in the local database chunks, and utilizes clean parametric memory fallback to present the latest known legal frameworks.
